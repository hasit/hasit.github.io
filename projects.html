VR for Wide-Field Ethnography
Capstone Thesis for Masters Degree at University of Washington Bothell. The purpose of this research is to investigate if and how a virtual reality (VR) head-mounted display (HMD) can be used for ethnographically-informed studies to produce better quality analysis than available by video analysis on desktop computer display.
Stress Detection, Recognition and Relief
My team - Om - proposed to use wearable sensors to monitor biological indicators of stress, and once elevated levels of stress are detected, a mitigation response would be suggested to the user. This project was proposed to IoT competition by Microsoft and University of Washington Bothell’s MakerSpace. We received an honourable mention of “All-Star Team”.

Eureka - A Physics App for Independant Learners
Project for Student Innovation Challenge at World Haptics Conference, Chicago 2015. An educational tool that uses Haptic Feedback to enhance the experience of learning the fundamentals of Physics through experiments. The tool consists of different modules, each prepares the user to excel in a field of understanding by allowing them to test theories in a simulated environment. The app allows users to simulate complex environments, compute forces acting on objects and predict the results. The user can construct different scenarios by placing objects, moving them across the screen and watching the environment react. They can adjust variables like gravity, weights of objects, elevation, and friction of surfaces. Haptic feedback is employed to simulate net force in order to give a more realistic experimentation when compared to conventional tools. Here is a video demo. This project was developed under the mentorship of Dr. Arjmand Samuel and Dr. Matt DePies
